width:autotakes into account the total width (ie: includes the padding/border/margin) in its calculation.

width:100%sets the content box to 100% of its parent's width, and does not take into account the padding/border/margin.
Note:If box-sizing:border-content is used, the total width accounts for padding/border. In this case, only margin is not accounted for when using width:100%

Open chrome dev tools and note that:

  • both childs are originally 600px wide (ie: same as width of its parent
  • when pbm is applied to child1, the content box is adjusted to account for the total width (ie: content + pbm = 600px)
  • when pbm is applied to child2, the content box is not adjusted, and the pbm are added onto the 600px content box (ie when no box-sizing: content(600px) + pbm = total width), resulting in overflow. With box-sizing (content+pb(600px) + m = total width)
